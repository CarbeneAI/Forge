### 2. Financial Statement Analysis

**Profitability Ratios:**
- Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- Operating Margin = Operating Income / Revenue
- Net Margin = Net Income / Revenue
- EBITDA Margin = EBITDA / Revenue
- Return on Assets (ROA) = Net Income / Total Assets
- Return on Equity (ROE) = Net Income / Shareholders' Equity
- Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) = NOPAT / Invested Capital

**Liquidity Ratios:**
- Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- Quick Ratio = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
- Cash Ratio = Cash & Equivalents / Current Liabilities
- Operating Cash Flow Ratio = Operating Cash Flow / Current Liabilities

**Leverage Ratios:**
- Debt-to-Equity = Total Debt / Total Equity
- Debt-to-Assets = Total Debt / Total Assets
- Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expense
- Debt Service Coverage = Operating Income / Total Debt Service

**Efficiency Ratios:**
- Asset Turnover = Revenue / Average Total Assets
- Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) = (Accounts Receivable / Revenue) × 365
- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) = (Inventory / COGS) × 365
- Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) = (Accounts Payable / COGS) × 365
- Cash Conversion Cycle = DSO + DIO - DPO

**Valuation Ratios:**
- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) = Market Price / EPS
- Price-to-Book (P/B) = Market Price / Book Value per Share
- Price-to-Sales (P/S) = Market Cap / Revenue
- EV/Revenue = Enterprise Value / Revenue
- EV/EBITDA = Enterprise Value / EBITDA
- PEG Ratio = P/E / Earnings Growth Rate

## Frameworks & Methodologies

### DuPont Analysis
ROE decomposition into three components:
- ROE = Net Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier
- ROE = (Net Income/Revenue) × (Revenue/Assets) × (Assets/Equity)

Identifies whether ROE is driven by profitability, efficiency, or leverage.

### Altman Z-Score (Bankruptcy Prediction)
Z = 1.2×(WC/TA) + 1.4×(RE/TA) + 3.3×(EBIT/TA) + 0.6×(MVE/TL) + 1.0×(Sales/TA)
- Z > 2.99: Safe zone
- 1.81 < Z < 2.99: Grey zone
- Z < 1.81: Distress zone

### Porter's Five Forces (Financial Implications)
Assess industry structure impact on profitability:
- Supplier power → Gross margin pressure
- Buyer power → Pricing power limits
- New entrants → Market share erosion
- Substitutes → Revenue risk
- Rivalry → Margin compression

### Working Capital Optimization
- Cash conversion cycle minimization
- Inventory optimization (EOQ, JIT)
- Receivables management (credit policy, collections)
- Payables optimization (payment terms, early pay discounts)

## Red Flags to Watch

### Financial Statement Red Flags
- Revenue growing faster than cash collections (DSO expanding)
- Gross margin deterioration without explanation
- Operating expenses growing faster than revenue
- Frequent accounting policy changes
- Large non-recurring items every quarter
- Working capital consuming cash despite profitability

### Business Model Red Flags
- Customer concentration (>20% revenue from single customer)
- Negative unit economics (LTV < CAC)
- CAC payback period extending
- High customer churn (>5% monthly for SaaS)
- Delayed revenue recognition patterns
- Declining pricing power

### Cash Flow Red Flags
- Operating cash flow negative despite profitable P&L
- Large gap between EBITDA and cash flow
- Increasing dependence on external financing
- Burn rate accelerating without revenue growth
- Insufficient runway (<12 months)
- Failed covenants or payment defaults

### Strategic Red Flags
- Acquisition-driven growth without organic growth
- Margin compression in mature markets
- R&D spending declining while competitors increase
- Key talent departures (especially finance team)
- Weak internal controls or audit findings
- Related party transactions without clear business rationale
